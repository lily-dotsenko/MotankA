The Traditions of Motanka

Today, surely every Ukrainian knows something or has heard of the ancient amulet of our ancestors – the MOTANKA.

What is a Motanka?

I shall not recount the findings of research, or indeed the creative struggles of scholars of antiquity, regarding the origins of motanka dolls and their sacred significance for our ancestors. Modern historical science is based on archaeological and theological materials but is largely shaped by the assumptions and conjectures of researchers – what exact role and significance 'motanka dolls' held in the ancient social life of our ancestors, the Trypillians and Oryans.

However, from those echoes of the past preserved in the genetic memory of our ancient people, one thing is clear: the antiquity of motanka dolls has its roots in the times of the inception of cultural cultivation and farming flax and hemp, where women played a key role.

It is well established that, within the families and society of the agrarian civilisation of our ancestors, the Orives, wives held a position equal to that of their husbands, and in certain spheres of life — such as domestic affairs, family life and the upbringing of children — they enjoyed a privileged status. As a rule, the eldest woman in the clan or family was 'VID-MA', that is, a wise, healing mother and guardian who knew how to treat and protect all her descendants from all that was unseen, negative and evil.

The modern Ukrainian woman, regardless of education, religion, or social status, is, on a subconscious level, that genetic, all-knowing Mother-Guardian for her loved ones!

How is an ancient talisman born in the modern world?

Every motanka knows whom it will serve, who needs help, who needs a helper, and who needs protection and a talisman.

When I take in my hands the components from which the motanka will be made, from the very first steps of selecting materials and details, from the very first strands — I sense the character and disposition, the resemblance to the person for whom the final creation will hold sacred meaning. Depending on its character and category, the doll — during the process of its creation — suggests to the craftswoman the details of the clothing, colours and symbols that it specifically requires.

For the creation of each motanka's body, a specific time, hour and mood are chosen, and until a certain part of the work is completed, the craftswoman does not rise from her table.

The Rule of the Moon

There is a strict rule regarding the day and the moon's position in the sky:

  • 🌘
    Purification Motanka — made during the waning moon
  • 🌒
    Helper Doll and dolls associated with good fortune — made during the waxing moon
  • 🌕
    Amulets — made during the full moon
  • ✦
    There is a special motanka made only a few days a year; it is a talisman intended to restore a person's destiny…

Materials & Craftsmanship

One of the most important factors in making a genuine motanka is the filling of the body with a mixture of herbs and twigs, and the choice of fabric for the body and clothing. The main fabric I use is antique, and above all, hand-woven, homespun linen and hemp.

A high-quality motanka amulet takes three to five days to make, and sometimes, as with 'The Happy Family', up to a week. There are both general and 'personalised' versions, which are made and charmed, filled with power — serving as a talisman for a specific person.

Family, ancestral, couple and personal motanka amulets require the most time and energy.
